Ecology, biogeography, and demography.

A Thriving and Diverse Population

Peru is home to approximately 34 million people, with a dynamic and growing population. The majority live in urban areas, especially in the vibrant city of Lima, which is home to over 11 million residents. The blend of mestizo, indigenous, and other cultural groups creates a rich, diverse society. Peru boasts a life expectancy of 77 years, reflecting the country’s advancements in healthcare and well-being.

Peru’s Incredible Wildlife

Peru is a paradise for animal lovers, offering a breathtaking range of species. In the Amazon rainforest, encounter awe-inspiring creatures like jaguars, sloths, and colorful macaws. The Andes are home to the iconic vicuña and majestic Andean condor, symbols of the country’s natural heritage. Along the coast, explore protected habitats where Humboldt penguins and sea lions thrive, making Peru a must-visit for wildlife enthusiasts.

The Rich Flora of Peru

Peru is a treasure trove of plant life, from the lush Amazon to the high Andes. In the Amazon, discover the exotic aguaje fruit and towering mahogany trees that sustain the region’s biodiversity. The Andes are the birthplace of ancient crops like quinoa and potatoes, which have been essential to local cultures for thousands of years. Peru’s coastal mangroves protect a wealth of wildlife, making them crucial to the country’s environmental balance.
